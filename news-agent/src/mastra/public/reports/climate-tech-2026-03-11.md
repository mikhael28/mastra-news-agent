## Breakthrough Tech & Big Deployments
*Machines, magnets, and megawatts — DAC wizardry, floating data islands, and reactors getting the green light*

- **DAC Breakthrough Unlocks 3x Cheaper Carbon Removal Projects (Sustaera)**  
  Sustaera unveiled a third-generation, electrically powered DAC system with reported >90% energy efficiency, projecting more than 3x cost reduction for carbon removal projects versus prior generations. If validated at scale, this could materially shift the economics of permanent CO₂ removal and accelerate project pipelines.  
  Source: portal.sina.com.hk — [Source](https://portal.sina.com.hk/finance/finance-globenewswire/globenewswire/2026/03/09/1593974/dac-breakthrough-unlocks-3x-cheaper-carbon-removal-projects/)

- **Bill Gates’ TerraPower Gets NRC Approval to Build New Nuclear Reactor**  
  TerraPower received Nuclear Regulatory Commission approval to construct a new reactor, a major regulatory milestone for advanced nuclear as a low-carbon, reliable generation option. The green light advances the case for nuclear in deep decarbonization strategies, though timelines and cost control will remain central questions.  
  Source: TechCrunch — [Source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/06/bill-gates-terrapower-gets-approval-to-build-new-nuclear-reactor/)

- **Aikido Launches Offshore Datacenter Technology to Deploy GW-scale Sovereign AI Compute**  
  Aikido Technologies introduced AO60DC, a floating-wind integrated platform combining multi-megawatt AI compute with wind turbines and batteries, aiming to deliver sovereign, low-carbon AI infrastructure offshore. The concept merges renewable power directly with compute demand, but will face engineering, regulatory, and O&M challenges before wide adoption.  
  Source: rss.globenewswire.com — [Source](https://rss.globenewswire.com/fr/news-release/2026/03/03/3248183/0/en/Aikido-Launches-Offshore-Datacenter-Technology-to-Deploy-GW-scale-Sovereign-AI-Compute-Onboard-Their-Floating-Wind-Platform.html)

- **Electrified Mineral-Based DAC Approach Resists Oxygen and Humidity**  
  Researchers report a durable, electrified mineral-based DAC system that resists degradation from oxygen and humidity — two common pain points for many sorbent-based DAC technologies. Increased operational robustness could lower lifecycle costs and broaden viable deployment geographies for DAC.  
  Source: TechXplore — [Source](https://techxplore.com/news/2026-02-durable-air-capture-approach-electrified.html)

- **Fortescue Advances 'Real Zero' With New Solar Farm Construction**  
  Fortescue has started construction on another large solar farm as part of its Pilbara Energy Connect initiative, moving closer to its "Real Zero" industrial power ambitions and further expanding high-voltage transmission in the Pilbara. For heavy industry players, such utility-scale builds are crucial to slashing scope 2 and scope 3 emissions.  
  Source: im-mining.com — [Source](https://im-mining.com/2026/03/10/fortescue-edges-closer-to-real-zero-ambitions-with-construction-of-latest-solar-farm/)

Policy shifts like this illustrate the dilution of fossil subsidies as a lever for near-term emissions reductions.

## On-the-Ground Projects & Sector Risks
*Dirt, data, and decarbonization — farmland turns into power plants, mines mind their metrics, and steam goes smart*

- **Climate Is Agriculture’s Biggest Business Risk**  
  Agriculture faces existential business risks from climate volatility, with producers and supply chains increasingly exposed to extreme weather and shifting growing conditions. The piece underscores the need for climate adaptation, insurance innovations, and on-farm mitigation measures to protect both yields and investors.  
  Source: www.sustainableviews.com — [Source](https://www.sustainableviews.com/climate-is-agricultures-biggest-business-risk-5006cc87/)

- **Turning Idle California Farmland Into A Clean Energy Powerhouse (Forbes)**  
  California projects are converting idle farmland into large-scale clean energy assets — solar farms, storage, and transmission that tap underused land for state decarbonization goals while sparking debates about land use and food security. These initiatives show how multi-benefit approaches and careful siting are essential to balance energy production with agricultural needs.  
  Source: www.forbes.com — [Source](https://www.forbes.com/sites/current-climate/2026/03/09/growing-a-clean-energy-powerhouse-on-idle-california-farmland/)

- **New Episode: Sustainable Reporting in Mining (Power Technology)**  
  A new podcast episode explores sustainability reporting in mining, featuring the Global Reporting Initiative and discussing transparency, metrics, and stakeholder expectations. Mining’s reporting evolution will be critical for investors and communities as demand for low-carbon minerals grows.  
  Source: www.power-technology.com — [Source](https://www.power-technology.com/news/new-episode-sustainable-reporting-in-mining/)

- **Carbon Direct Calls for Integrating Superpollutant Mitigation With Carbon Removal Strategy**  
  Carbon Direct released a paper arguing that superpollutant mitigation (methane, HFCs, black carbon) must be integrated with long-term carbon removal strategies to optimize near- and long-term temperature outcomes. The report advocates a portfolio approach that balances rapid warming reduction with durable CO₂ removal.  
  Source: finance.yahoo.com — [Source](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/carbon-direct-releases-groundbreaking-paper-131000064.html)

- **Imperium Technologies Secures $2M to Decarbonize Industrial Steam Infrastructure**  
  Imperium Technologies raised $2M to advance AI-driven monitoring and optimization of industrial steam systems, targeting a sector responsible for a large share of industrial fossil fuel use. Smarter steam operations can be a high-impact, cost-effective decarbonization lever for heavy industry.
